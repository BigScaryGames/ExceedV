## Context

The current free-map movement system is breaking gameplay at higher AP tiers (10+ AP), allowing excessive movement. The goal is to transition to a **Lines and Zones** system inspired by FATE's abstract positioning.

### Current Problems
- Variable AP with free-map movement allows too much movement at high tiers
- Doesn't support anime-style, free-form combat aesthetics
- Grid was versatile but AP costs allow too much action per turn at higher levels

### New System Overview

**Zones**: Abstract areas (rooms, alleyways, squares, city blocks, villages). At higher levels/superheroic campaigns, zones scale (buildings, city blocks). GM decides zoning based on scene needs.

**Lines**: Connections between zones. Movement cost = `ceil((zone_distance × modifiers) / speed)`. Max 20 cost (4 movement × 5 default actions), ideally capped at 10 in early game. **Must have full AP to cross - no partial movement.**

**In-Zone Movement**: Takes 1 action (Engage) to move to "engage distance" of an enemy.

**Engage Distance**: Melee without reach. Reach weapons can target without engaging.

**Ranged Attacks**: Can target any zone with line of sight. Use **sum of line distances** for penalty calculation. Example: Arena has 3 zones with 2 lines of 10m each = 20m total distance. This might mean -2 penalty, or 2 Aim actions to negate.

**Off-Guard**: No flanking mechanic. Triggered by explicit effects/abilities. Ganging up uses numeric advantage system.

**Reactions**: No longer consume next turn's AP.

**New AP System**: `5 + DomainTiers` base AP, with extra AP domain-restricted:
- `#combat` actions (strikes, martial skills)
- `#spellcraft` actions (casting spells)
- Consider: `#ranged` as separate domain in future

**Movement Action Changes**:
- **Step → Engage**: 1 AP, move to engage distance within a zone, replaces Step
- **Stride → Zone Movement**: Cost varies based on line distance/speed, repurposed for zone-to-zone movement
- **Disengage**: New action that prevents reactions when leaving engagement
- **Leaving engagement without Disengage**: Triggers enemy reactions/effects

**Ganging Up (Numeric Advantage)**:
- One side has more combatants engaged than the other
- -1 to deflect/dodge per disadvantage to the outnumbered side
- When 4+ allies are in one engagement, they no longer suffer numeric disadvantage (back-to-back rule)

## Edge Cases & Solutions (ALL RESOLVED)

### 1. Large Zones with Multiple Enemies
**Logic**: Engagement groups. If A engages B who is engaged with C, A engages both B and C.
**Possibilities**:
- Separate 1v1 duels
- 2v2 engagement
- 2v1 with archer at back (not engaged)
**Future**: Perks to force engagement (bodyblocking), perks to deny engaging ally

### 2. Flying/Swimming
**Logic**: Same zone if not too far. If further, separate zone that requires flying to enter.

### 3. Zone Barriers with Checks
**Logic**: Requires AP. On failure, stay in original zone. If multiple successes/barrier, can attack/interact from both sides. Resolve checks and spend actions, then cross.

### 4. Chase Scenes
**Logic**: Separate zone-based mechanics, without engagement rules. (Future work)

### 5. Stealth in Combat
**Logic**: Can't be engaged to hide. Hide action with DC = highest Perception DC + number of enemies.
**On success**: Attack with off-guard OR sneak to another zone.
**Stealth assassination perk**: If fails, no longer hidden.

### 6. Zone Destruction
**Logic**: Delete zone from map. Destroy or move actors to new/existing zones.

### 7. AoE Spells
**Logic**: Two types:
- **Zonal**: Acts on whole zone. Check distance/spell range to hit other zones.
- **Targeted**: Selectively target enemies. (Needs separate discussion)
**Additional**: Can cast on lines to give them effects.

### 8. Cover
**Logic**: Zone effects like "[[Fortified Defenders]]" giving bonuses to defenders. Offenders can carry equipment (siege equipment) for similar effects.

### 9. Crowded Zones
**Logic**: Zone effect. Either:
- Penalty to reach/large weapon users
- **CHOSEN**: Auto-engage on entry, can't disengage

### 10. 1D Zones (Narrow Corridors)
**Logic**: Special zone effect - only 1 combatant allowed, automatically engaged.

### 11. Reach Weapons
**Logic**: Cannot engage adjacent zones. Can only attack without entering engagement (e.g., helping ally).

### 12. Shoving
**Logic**: Forcefully disengage target from combat (no zone movement).

### 13. Partial Movement on Lines
**Logic**: Must have full AP to cross; no partial movement.

### 14. Ranged Attack Penalties
**Logic**: Use sum of line distances. 20m distance = -2 penalty or 2 Aim actions.

### 15. Ganging Up (Numeric Advantage)
**Logic**: -1 to deflect/dodge per disadvantage. 4+ allies in engagement = no disadvantage (back-to-back rule).

### 16. Engage/Disengage Costs
**Logic**: Engage = 1 AP (replaces Step). Disengage = prevents reactions. Leaving without disengage triggers reactions.
