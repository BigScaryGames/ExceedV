## Legal Rights and Protections

Different ranks provide specific legal privileges within their domains:

**Inquisitor:** Legal authority to investigate supernatural threats. Can requisition aid from local authorities. Protected under religious law.

**Licensed Healer:** Medical immunity when providing care. Can request authority to quarantine during outbreaks.

**Nobility:** Trial by peers. Immunity from arrest by lower-ranking officials. Authority to levy taxes and conscript militia.

**Guild (Rank 2+):** Journeymen work independently. Masters have authority over apprentices, voting rights. Grandmasters arbitrate disputes.

**Military Officer (Rank 3+):** Authority over troops. Can requisition supplies. May impose martial law in emergencies.

## Rules of Hospitality

Rank 2+ can expect basic accommodations from peers by asking a favor.

**Noble Hospitality:** Must provide appropriate accommodation to visiting peers. Breach is a serious social offense.

**Religious Sanctuary:** Ordained clergy can provide sanctuary in temples and holy sites.

**Academic Courtesy:** Access to libraries up to your rank, free attendance at lectures, exam-period accommodations.

**Military Courtesy:** Officers from non-hostile forces are entitled to parley rights and honorable treatment.

## Recognition and Verification

- **Visual:** Uniforms, badges, seals, heraldry, ceremonial items
- **Documentation:** Papers, writs, patents, licenses, certificates
- **Knowledge Tests:** Passwords, codes, rituals, specialized knowledge
- **Reputation:** High-ranking individuals known by sight in their sphere

## Cross-Organizational Rank

- Military defers to higher-equivalent nobility
- Religious authority supersedes civilian rank in matters of faith
- Academic rank carries weight in scholarly circles regardless of social standing
