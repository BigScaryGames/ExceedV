The maps in Exceed are defined in Lines and Zones. Maps can be used with or without grid as distances are tracked with lines, so no map, or a map on a napkin would work as well as any other map.

A creature’s position amounts to two things: the creature’s whereabouts (what Zone it is in), and who it’s contending or shielding with in melee.(Skirmish)
# Zones
Within a zone, you can get into [[Skirmish]] with an enemy or an ally with a single [[Engage]] action, followed by an attack or other offensive ability. 

When you are engaging an enemy or an ally or an enemy or an ally [[Engage]]s you, you engage all the targets in the [[Skirmish]].

On a tactical map put your token on your side of the encounter and enemy next to yourself. If enemy is in a skirmish already, put your token on your side of the skirmish. That doesn't represent the real positioning.


# Lines
Lines represent the distance between zones and have a statistic of distance and can have effects on them.
Distance * Multipliers from Effects = Cost of traversal. To calculate how much actions you need to move from Zone A to Zone B, take the Cost / speed and ceiling it. i.e. Actions=Distance * Multiplier/Speed.
Advised to GM to write the costs on the map next to the line to keep track of the effects.

! Rule of thumb
Lines should cost no more than 20 in the late game and no more than 10 in early game.

To calculate distance between disconnected zones reference [[Distance in Combat]].
# Effects
Lines and Zones can hold effects on them that modify the rules, providing effects, abilities and alike.

Environmental, situational or magical some can be removed, suppressed or bypassed with an abilities explicitly stating that and with checks or improvisation approved by the GM.

Examples:
Line is a wall, requiring DC 15 climbing check and distance 10.
*"Hey GM, i want to cast levitate on the group"/"Hey GM, i want to be a big guy and help others climb by jump starting them"/"Hey GM, can I throw my rope dart and climb that way"*
*"Cast the spell/Lower the DC by 5 if you spend your whole turn on this/Add an extra AP, and reduce DC by 3."*

Default on Effects in [[Lines and Zones Effects]].
