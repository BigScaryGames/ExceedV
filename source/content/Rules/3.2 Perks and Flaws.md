# What Are Perks?
Perks represent specific training, experiences, conditions or specializations your character has undergone. They embody the "how" and "why" behind your character's capabilities.

Perks cost XP and represent investments in specific areas of expertise. When you take a perk, you typically gain attribute points equal to the XP spent (some perks are free and some are without an attribute). These attribute points bring selected attributes closer to their next threshold (positive or negative).

Most perks don't have levels, but some do. In that case each previous level overrides each next level, as per perk description.

Perks may have prerequisites (other perks, skills, or attributes) and provide mechanical benefits that reflect your character's specialized training.

## Perk Categories

- **Combat Perks**: Specialized combat techniques, weapon training, tactical abilities
- **Skill Perks**: Specialized applications of skills (though skill perks don't directly increase skill levels)
- **Spellcraft Perks**: Magical specializations and techniques

## How Perks Work

1. Spend Combat XP or Skill XP to purchase a perk
2. Choose which attribute receive the XP points (following the perk's specifications, if any)
3. The XP spent becomes attribute points that move the chosen attribute toward the next threshold
4. Example: Spend 5 XP on a combat perk, choose Might → Might gains 5 points toward +1 threshold

Perks follow the ability/effect system structure where abilities are active choices and effects are passive bonuses.
